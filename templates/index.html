Public WiFi Safety Guidelines

Essential practices to protect yourself on unfamiliar networks

Avoid Open / Unsecured Networks
Never connect to networks with no password. All traffic is visible in plaintext to anyone on the same network.
Verify Network Authenticity
Before connecting, ask staff for the exact SSID. If TwinGuard flags a network you're about to join, walk away.
Use HTTPS Sites Only
Always verify the padlock in your browser. Avoid banking or email on public WiFi — sessions can be intercepted.
Use a VPN on Public Networks
A VPN encrypts all traffic, neutralising most man-in-the-middle attacks even on rogue access points.
Disable File & Printer Sharing
Turn off network discovery in OS settings on public networks to block access to your shared resources.
Forget Networks After Use
Remove public WiFi profiles after use so your device doesn't silently reconnect to a spoofed twin later.
